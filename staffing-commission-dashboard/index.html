The problem

The commission sheet is where trust goes to die

A recruiter pings you: “My number’s wrong.” You open the file on a Friday afternoon and you’re reconciling draws against earned commission by hand, chasing which placements are still inside the guarantee window, trying to remember who owned the client versus who sourced the candidate.

Questions people ask

Staffing commission tracking, answered

How do staffing agencies track commissions?
Most start in a spreadsheet — perm placements calculated as a percentage of first-year salary, contract placements as a percentage of the bill-pay spread, with splits, draws, and guarantee windows tracked by hand. That works until it doesn’t: month-end reconciliation eats a Friday and recruiters stop trusting their number. The reliable version is a live commission dashboard where those rules are written down once and the numbers update themselves. That’s what I build — $650 flat, and the first slice is free through the 1-day mini-demo.

How do I stop commission disputes on a recruiting desk?
Disputes almost always come from two places: split placements (who owned the client vs. who sourced the candidate) and clawbacks (a candidate quits inside the guarantee window and a paid commission has to be reversed). You stop them by settling both by written rule in one dashboard every recruiter can see — splits calculated automatically, at-risk placements flagged before payout. When the math is transparent and self-updating, “my number’s wrong” turns into a link instead of an argument.
Can you build a recruiter commission tracker in Google Sheets?

It’s a live, self-updating commission dashboard in Google Sheets: per-recruiter earned/pending/paid, perm and contract handled separately, splits settled by rule, draw balances reconciled, clawback flags on at-risk placements, and a payout-ready month-end summary. It runs alongside your ATS rather than replacing it. $650 flat, and you can see a working slice on your own numbers first, free.
How do you handle clawbacks and fall-offs in commission tracking?
Every placement inside its guarantee window (30, 60, or 90 days, however your desk sets it) is flagged as at-risk right on the dashboard. If a candidate falls off, the commission that was already earned or paid is reversed against that recruiter’s balance instead of quietly slipping through. You see the exposure before you cut the check, so a fall-off is a flag you planned for, not a surprise you eat.
Can it handle split desks and draws against commission?
Yes — those are the two hardest parts, so they’re built in. Splits are calculated by whatever rule your desk agreed (client-owner share vs. candidate-sourcer share), automatically, on every shared placement. Draws are netted against earned commission so each recruiter’s real balance — including what they owe back if they under-earn against a guarantee — is shown plainly. No more reconciling either one by hand at month-end.
